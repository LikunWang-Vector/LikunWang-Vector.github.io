## Overview

This research project focuses on developing a comprehensive framework for predicting loading forces applied to fish vertebrae structures using a combination of topology optimization and deep neural networks.

## Methodology

### Data Acquisition
- Used CT scans to acquire high-precision 3D data of fish vertebrae
- Generated detailed geometric models for finite element analysis

### Finite Element Analysis
- Implemented COMSOL Multiphysics for structural analysis
- Combined with topology optimization to explore lightweight structural properties
- Investigated bio-inspired design principles

### Deep Learning Integration
- Developed deep neural network models to predict loading forces
- Achieved over 20% improvement in calculation efficiency and accuracy
- Integrated machine learning with traditional engineering analysis

## Key Achievements

- **Improved Accuracy**: Enhanced prediction precision by over 20%
- **Computational Efficiency**: Significantly reduced calculation time through ML integration
- **Conference Presentation**: Oral presentation at WCSMO-16 (May 2025)
- **Interdisciplinary Approach**: Successfully combined biomechanics, optimization, and AI

## Technical Stack

- **Simulation**: COMSOL Multiphysics
- **Programming**: Python, MATLAB
- **Deep Learning**: PyTorch/TensorFlow
- **Data Processing**: NumPy, Pandas
- **Visualization**: Matplotlib, Scientific plotting tools

## Impact

This research contributes to the understanding of bio-inspired structural design and demonstrates the potential of combining traditional engineering methods with modern AI techniques for enhanced predictive capabilities in biomechanical systems.
